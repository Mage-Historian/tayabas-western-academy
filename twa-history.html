ABOUT TAYABAS WESTERN ACADEMY

BRIEF HISTORY OF TAYABAS WESTERN ACADEMY

The TAYABAS WESTERN ACADEMY was founded in 1928 by the late Mr. Iñigo Javier with aid of Mr. Vicente Vilar of Lopez Quezon. This school was opened in June 1928 in a rented house at the corner of Cabuñag and Bustamante Streets. The enrolment then was only forty composed of Freshmen and Sophomores.

The Old Tayabas Western Academy
Between Cabuñag and De Gala Streets, Brgy. Poblacion, Candelaria, Quezon.
Photo by: Candelaria Tourism Facebook Page.

In November 1931, Judge Severino B. De Gala was elected President of the Board of Trustees. That year, the TWA suffered a financial setback. For four months, the instructors were not paid their salaries while the school was under foreclosure for obligations long overdue. The permanent recognition of the school could not be granted by the office of Private Education for lack of equipment. The school was under a critical condition.

At the close of SY 1931-1932, Mr. Honorio Montecillo was appointed School Director. He began to rehabilitate the school. The debts were paid, equipment acquired, and finally the permanent recognition of the General Secondary Course was granted. During this school year, the first Graduation Exercises was held for Class 1932.

The History of TWA will not be complete without mentioning its services extended to the public from 1949 to 1954, a two-year junior Normal Collegiare Course and a complete Elementary Course were offered during these years.

In S.Y. 1965-66, the school applied for the opening of college courses. In June 1967, the College Department was opened offering BSE, BSC, AB, and General Clerical Course. There were 150 students. TWA temporarily stopped operation from 1990-1991.

In SY 1993-1994, the TWA in all levels re-opened to serve the community.

In 2009, the school offered pre-elementary [N.K.P] and Grades 1-2.

In 2010-2011, TWA offered Grade 3 and 4.

Then in 2011-2012, TWA offered a complete elementary course.
